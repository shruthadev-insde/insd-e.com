What This Is

[INSD]e* is not an architecture studio. It is not a consultancy. It is not a research lab. It is all of these simultaneously.

Inquiries in Strategic Design is a multidisciplinary practice based in Mangaluru, structured across seven domains - Environments, Experience, Epistemics, Education, Experiments, Exchanges, and eYE. Each domain is a different mode of engaging with the built world. A project may span more than one. The practice begins with inquiry before it arrives at form.

INSD-eYE is the aesthetic intelligence of the practice. It is where art is sourced for the spaces we design, where curated programming is developed, and where relationships are built with the makers, collectors, and designers whose work carries the kind of authenticity that cannot be manufactured. The art brief, for us, is never an afterthought. It is part of the spatial conversation from day one.

To be part of INSD-eYE is to operate at the intersection of spatial design and cultural production. It is to understand that a room is not finished when the furniture arrives. It is finished when everything in it - the object on the shelf, the textile underfoot, the work on the wall - has been chosen with the same precision as the wall itself.

Consulting the eye...

To the visual curator. The designer who thinks before they make. The one who walks into a room and immediately understands what it is missing - and why.

We are looking for someone specific. Not a generalist. Not someone who is good at everything. Someone who is extraordinary at this one thing: understanding how objects, art, and spatial intention speak to each other.

INSD-eYE needs a creative lead. Someone who will carry this domain of the practice forward - who will build the curatorial language, develop relationships with makers and collectors, and bring the aesthetic intelligence of eYE into every project [INSD]e* takes on. This is not a supporting role. It is a founding one, for the domain.

* What This Role Requires
A trained eye - in visual arts, interior design, spatial curation, or a field we have not thought to list here but that you know qualifies you.
The ability to hold a curatorial position and defend it. Not someone who agrees with the brief - someone who sharpens it.
A genuine relationship with craft and making. You do not need to make things yourself. But you need to understand the people who do.
Comfort working across spatial design, client conversations, and cultural programming simultaneously. eYE does not sit in one lane.
An understanding that restraint is a skill. The best curatorial decisions are often about what you leave out.

We are a young practice. We will not pretend otherwise. What we offer in return is creative authority over a domain that is still being built - the chance to define what INSD-eYE actually becomes. The work will be rigorous. The standards will be non-negotiable. The scope, for the right person, will be as large as their ambition.

To the maker, the collector, the one whose eye we trust.

If you are reading this, your work has already been noticed.

Not in passing. With attention.

We look for work that stays longer than most things do today. Work that carries the weight of a real hand, a real decision, a real history. If that describes what you do, this letter is for you.

Within the objects you create, there is a profound stillness - a form that has arrived after generations of consideration. Whether it is the grain of the wood or the temper of the metal, your work is not just decoration; it is conviction made visible.

I am Ar. Kajal Diwakar. I am the Creative Director at [INSD]e* - Inquiries in Strategic Design, a multidisciplinary design consultancy practice based in Mangalore. We do not believe design begins with a drawing. It begins with an inquiry - into site, into context, into the people a space is being made for.

eYE is where we source art for the spaces we design, develop curated programming, and build relationships with makers, collectors, and designers whose work carries the kind of authenticity that cannot be manufactured. The art brief, for us, is never an afterthought. It is part of the spatial conversation from day one.

We believe equally in the traditional and the contemporary, in craft that has been passed down through hands, and in work that is asking entirely new questions. What we look for, always, is intention. Work that is honest. Work that rewards a longer look.

This is why I am writing to you.

We are proposing a collaboration built on shared values. Remarkable work reaches further when carried by more than one voice. Your story and work would be introduced to our clients who trust us to curate the life within their spaces.
