THE CURRENT PLAN

Good Instinct.
Wrong Method.

THE INSTINCT

The developer executes the interiors. Quality control stays in-house. Brand coherence is maintained. Speed of delivery is predictable. This is exactly right.

THE METHOD

Two or three cost-based templates. Materials sourced from China. Pre-fabricated and assembled on booking. Fast, cheap to spec, easy to repeat.

THE PROBLEM

A template signals compromise to a premium buyer. At ₹1.3 to 1.6 crores for a 3BHK in a 60-floor sea-view building, the buyer's expectation is not a catalogue. The moment the interior feels off-the-shelf, they bring their own designer. Quality control disappears. Brand coherence breaks. A significant revenue stream is abandoned.

THE TEMPLATE MODEL IS THE RIGHT INSTINCT EXECUTED THE WRONG WAY.
THERE IS A BETTER STRUCTURE — ONE THAT GIVES THE DEVELOPER COMPLETE
CONTROL AND GIVES THE RESIDENT THE FEELING OF GENUINE AUTHORSHIP.

THE PROPOSAL

Not Interior Design.
A Design Intelligence System.

A framework that generates personalised, coherent, premium interiors at scale — across 296 units, across every future Yamuna development, without sacrificing quality control or brand integrity.

A 5-step guided journey: persona identified through questionnaire, palette selected (3 recommended, all 9 available), register confirmed by budget, rooms configured with presets, brief delivered.

THE HOST

The host who lets the architecture do the talking.

Luxury in weight, not decoration. The Host has always known what a room should feel like — resolved, complete, composed. Three palettes, one conviction: the home is the statement. Stone & Shadow. Tobacco & Dusk. Noir & Amber.

THE SEEKER

The seeker who never stopped looking, even after coming home.

Art-led, layered, unapologetic. Every object has a provenance, a journey. The Seeker curates for no one but themselves. White Walls & Wandered Objects. Grey Plaster & Living Art. Grand & Indian.

THE SETTLER

The warmth that moved with them.

They are not starting over. They are continuing. The familiarity they have always lived in has simply arrived at a new address. Warm, genuine, deeply theirs. Their home feels like it has always been this way.

These are not demographic segments. They are not aesthetic preferences. They are identities.
The moment a resident reads their persona, they recognise themselves.

HOW THE SYSTEM WORKS

Same Palette.
Same Register.
Three Completely Different Homes.

The persona does not change what materials are available. It changes how they are curated.

The same palette and register, interpreted through three persona filters, produces three homes that feel genuinely different — in their spatial grammar, their density of objects, their degree of resolution, and their emotional temperature.

eXM — THE DIGITAL PRODUCT

Yamuna Sky · Accord

An interior configurator built as a resident experience, not a selection tool.

Accord is the digital interface through which each resident composes their home. It guides them through five considered decisions — persona, palette, register, surfaces, living character, lighting and artwork — and assembles a coherent, personalised interior brief that maps directly to a specification and a delivery SKU.

For the resident, it turns a potentially anxious post-purchase decision into a moment of genuine creative agency.

For Yamuna, it turns the interior offering into a pre-sales engagement tool — something residents look forward to before possession rather than worry about after it.

Accord is delivered to Yamuna and owned by Yamuna. It becomes a permanent asset of the developer's brand.

01 — BRAND LEVERAGE

Volume creates negotiating power.

296 units of guaranteed specification is a serious number for any brand partner. Kajaria, Kohler, Duravit, Poliform, Porcelanosa will negotiate significant trade pricing for a committed placement at this scale. The system's margins improve as brand partnerships deepen.

02 — RESALE COHERENCE

The building's design language protects value.

A home whose aesthetic belongs to the building holds value differently from one whose aesthetic belongs to the previous occupant. Every Sky City Accord interior is a version of Yamuna's language, not a personal statement. That coherence survives ownership transitions.

03 — MADE IN INDIA

The sourcing story is a PR asset.

Indian craft studios, commissioned artwork by Karnataka-based artists, brand partnerships with Indian luxury manufacturers. In 2026, "crafted in India" is a narrative that architectural and lifestyle press will pick up. No China-sourced template model can claim it.

04 — COMPOUNDING VALUE

Designed once. Deployed at scale.

The Sky City Accord system is designed for Yamuna Sky City and owned by Yamuna Homes and Design. Every subsequent development in the Yamuna portfolio can activate Accord with a new palette set. The design investment of one project becomes the infrastructure of a developer brand.

eXP — SALES COLLATERAL

The Building Deserves
Better Marketing.

The current Yamuna Sky City brochure presents a new launch. What this building needs is collateral that presents a home being lived in.

[INSD]e* proposes a completely new sales collateral system for the interior offering: photorealistic renders that show inhabited spaces — morning light on a kitchen counter, books on a shelf, a view through a bedroom window at dusk. The feeling of a life already happening here.

This is not a brochure redesign. It is a new visual identity for the interior offering that extends from the brochure to the sales office to the microsite.

This is what Yamuna Sky City could feel like from the inside.

THE PROBLEM

Choice isn't freedom.
Curation is.

A standard interior configurator presents hundreds of independent decisions across thousands of possible combinations. The resident, unfamiliar with material compatibility, proportion, or how a colour reads at 3 am, is asked to be their own designer. The result is anxiety, not authorship.

Accord works differently. The system makes the invisible decisions first — the ones that require design training — and surfaces only the choices that are genuinely expressive of who you are.

HOW THE SYSTEM WORKS

What the system
decides before you do.

Each layer of input collapses a set of decisions. By the time you reach the configurator, you are not choosing from hundreds of options — you are refining from a world that was already built for you.

THE SCOPE

Everything behind
what you see.

The configurable system covers every material layer of the home, every specification decision, and every brand relationship — from the grout between your floor tiles to the gallery that sources your artwork.
